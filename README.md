# CoreCraft

CoreCraft is a semi-lightweight Minecraft modpack, meaning there is some modded content to discover, while still maintaining many (improved) vanilla elements to give users a sense of familiarity while being able to run well on most machines. Here to making new experiences and core memories in our favourite block game!

### What does CoreCraft feature in your gameplay?

- 🗡️ **Better Combat**: Pick between a variety of classes that each have their own equipment and abilities. Enhance your power by unlocking new spells, uncovering new trinkets, and advancing through the skill tree. Dominate your opponents with combat roll, trinkets, and new enchantments.

- 🌎 **A Remodelled World**: Find overhauled vanilla structures, new structures and biomes loaded with new and existing mobs that still fits the vanilla feel. Whether you find jungle temples or sand temples filled with puzzles, traps, and treasure or find massive volcanoes and mountains, or even encounter battle arenas with bosses in them.

- 🚀 **Handpicked Quality of Life Features**: QOL features such as sort, loot, restock, quick stack in inventories, backpacks with abilities, being able to hold right-click to build fast, bone meal working for all crops, auto harvesting and planting with hoes, and much more.

- 👥 **Parties and Guilds**: Create parties and guilds with other players that disables accidental friendly fire on your teammates

- 🎤 **Proximity Voice Chat**: Talk to nearby players! But be careful as your voice can also trigger sculk sensors and can be heard by the warden

- 🎮 **Newbie Friendly**: Does installing a modpack seem daunting to you? No problem, we have a variety of mods to help new players like EMI mod which is an item and crafting recipe browser in your inventory, Jade mod to display information from blocks or entities that you are looking at, Controlling mod which helps you configure your keybinds, and much more!

# Recommendations

Our recommendations for an optimal experience:

- 💻 [Allocate](https://github.com/brucethemoose/Minecraft-Performance-Flags-Benchmarks?tab=readme-ov-file#memory-allocation) at least **3 GB of RAM** to the instance if playing single-player. More RAM allocated would be recommended for higher player counts and loading big structures
  
- 🗺️ Pregenerate chunks using [Chunky](https://github.com/pop4959/Chunky/wiki/Pregeneration). to avoid unnecessary lag loading chunks and to create clean looking map renders for the world. This guide also has a world size calculator to calculate the storage needed to generate the world size
  
- 🖥️ Cap your FPS to your monitor refresh rate and turn off V-sync
  
- ☕️ Setting Java installation to [Adoptium Temurin](https://adoptium.net/temurin)
  
- ⬇️ Install the modpack through [Modrinth App](https://modrinth.com/app) or [Prism Launcher](https://prismlauncher.org/) as it allows you to automatically install the modpack and configure microphone permissions for [Simple Voice Chat](https://modrepo.de/minecraft/voicechat/wiki/macos)
  
- 💾 Whenever you update the modpack, [backup your worlds](https://minecraft.wiki/w/World_Options#Java_Edition:~:text=Makes%20a%20new,the%20display%20name.) for good practice
  
- ⚙️ Copying the configureddefaults files to your main installation after each modpack update and enabling updated resourcepacks
  
- 💬 Feel free to join the [Discord server](https://discord.gg/Gwr9AdFEzz) for inquiries and to share core memories together
